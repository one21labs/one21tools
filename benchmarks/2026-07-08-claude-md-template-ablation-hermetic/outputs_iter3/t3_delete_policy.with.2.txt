Before that implementation happens, there are a few policy decisions worth pinning down first, since "delete account" for GDPR erasure isn't just a `DELETE FROM users` call:

- **Hard delete vs. anonymize** — full row deletion vs. scrubbing PII while keeping a stub row (needed if other tables FK to `users.id` for things like order history, audit logs, or financial records you're legally required to retain).
- **Cascading data** — what else references the user (posts, sessions, tokens, payment records, logs) and whether those cascade-delete, get anonymized, or are excluded under a retention exception.
- **Auth requirement** — re-authentication (password/OTP) before deletion, not just an authenticated session, to prevent session-hijack deletion.
- **Verification/audit trail** — GDPR erasure requests typically need proof of completion (a deletion log entry, timestamped, kept separately from the deleted user's data).
- **Timing** — immediate purge vs. a grace period (soft-delete flag + scheduled hard-delete job) to allow undo/abuse recovery.
- **Legal holds** — any records you must retain regardless of the erasure request (tax/financial data under other regs).
