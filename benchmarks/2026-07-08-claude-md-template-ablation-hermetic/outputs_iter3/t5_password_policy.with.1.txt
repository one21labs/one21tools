If you just want a reasonable default to start from, a commonly recommended policy (aligned with NIST 800-63B) is:
- Minimum 12 characters (no arbitrary max below ~64, and don't silently truncate)
- No forced composition rules (no "must have 1 uppercase, 1 symbol") — those push people toward predictable patterns
- Reject common/breached passwords (check against a list like the top 10k, or the Pwned Passwords API)
- Block passwords equal to the username/email
- Show real-time strength feedback client-side (e.g., zxcvbn) but **always re-validate server-side** — client checks are UX only, not security
