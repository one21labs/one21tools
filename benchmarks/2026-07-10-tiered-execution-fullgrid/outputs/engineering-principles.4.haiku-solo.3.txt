I'd recommend against writing this plan. You're operating on sunk-cost fallacy — the two weeks spent on over-architecture shouldn't drive the next two weeks of work. The requirement is straightforward (read JSON, validate three fields), and that's what your users need to unblock.

The cleaner path: finish the JSON implementation properly, get it shipped, then let actual user requests (not hypothetical convenience) drive the format adapters and hot-reload. You'll have the architecture to hang them on if/when someone asks.

If you want to spend the remaining sprint time productively on config, spend it on observability, error messages, testing coverage, or docs — things that make the shipped feature actually solid instead of finishing features nobody asked for.
