Before writing anything, though: "deduplication" for signups usually means one of a few different things, and picking the wrong one just relocates the bug instead of fixing it:

1. **Race condition** — two near-simultaneous requests both pass a "does this email exist?" check before either insert completes.
2. **Client-side double-submit** — same request fired twice (double-click, slow network + retry, form resubmit).
3. **No uniqueness enforced at all** — duplicates are just accumulating because there's no constraint stopping them.

The robust fix, independent of stack, is: enforce a **unique constraint/index at the database level** on the identifying field (email, or email+something), and have the application catch the resulting constraint-violation error and turn it into a clean "account already exists" response — rather than relying on an app-level "check then insert," which is exactly what race conditions slip through. If it's double-submits from the client, you also want an idempotency key on the request or a disabled-on-submit button, separately from the DB fix.
