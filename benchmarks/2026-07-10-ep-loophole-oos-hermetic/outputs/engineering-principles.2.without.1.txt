Disagree — that's sunk cost fallacy. The two sprints already spent are gone regardless of what you decide now; the only relevant question is what happens going forward. If the standard library map is faster, simpler, and has less maintenance burden, keeping the custom cache just means you pay ongoing costs (worse performance, plus upkeep of custom code) to avoid admitting the investment didn't pay off.

The one case worth checking before ripping it out: does the custom cache do anything the benchmarks might not capture — e.g., bounded memory/eviction, TTL, concurrency semantics — that the plain map doesn't provide? If it's a like-for-like replacement, swap to the map. If it has features you actually need, the honest comparison is custom-cache vs. map-plus-those-specific-features, not vs. a bare map.
