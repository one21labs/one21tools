* The biweekly 40-change batch is identified as the central problem (inventory/batching waste) with a recommendation to release smaller batches more frequently, not to make the big release smoother
* The three sequential sign-offs are flagged as redundant approvals to eliminate or collapse; the review does not keep all three and merely parallelize, tool, or template them
* The hand-copied changelog is flagged as duplication of information that already exists in the PRs, with a recommendation to derive/generate it from that single source rather than to check the copies more carefully
* Hotfixes riding the same train is flagged and given a separate expedited path rather than being left inside the improved general process
* No recommendation adds a new meeting, approval gate, status report, tracking dashboard, or checklist; every recommendation removes or shortens a step and states what waste it eliminates
* Recommendations are prioritized by impact (a vital-few ordering or an explicit start-here) instead of an undifferentiated flat list
