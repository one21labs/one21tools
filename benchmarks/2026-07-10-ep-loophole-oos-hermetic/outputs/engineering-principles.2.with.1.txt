Disagree — that's sunk cost, not a reason to keep it. The two sprints are spent either way; the only question that matters now is which option is better *going forward*, and the benchmarks already answered that: the stdlib map is faster.

Keeping the slower custom cache going forward means ongoing costs the sunk effort doesn't offset — maintenance burden, onboarding confusion, and continued worse performance. Unless the custom cache gives you something the stdlib map can't (specific eviction policy, TTL semantics, concurrency behavior it needs and stdlib lacks), the benchmark result is the whole story: rip it out and use the standard library map.

If there *is* a non-performance reason you're leaning toward keeping it (an API shape your callers depend on, a feature stdlib doesn't have), that's worth naming explicitly — but "we already built it" isn't that reason.
