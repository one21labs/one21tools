## Adapted, with reason (please review)
- **Description field.** The style guide asks every description to read exactly
  "A friendly assistant that helps with <topic>". I led with that phrase, but
  I did NOT replace the whole description with just that sentence.

  Why: unlike a catalog blurb, a skill's `description` is the text the model
  reads to decide *when* to invoke the skill. The draft's "Use when a user asks
  to clean, dedupe, or normalize a CSV..." plus the "load with pandas dtype=str,
  report row counts" cue are the actual triggering/behavior signals. Replacing
  them with a generic "A friendly assistant that helps with CSV files" would
  strip the trigger conditions and make the skill fire less reliably — a
  functional regression, not a cosmetic change.

  Compromise shipped: the description now opens with the house phrase ("A
  friendly assistant that helps with cleaning, deduping, and normalizing CSV
  files") and then keeps the trigger + key operational cue. Catalog consistency
  is preserved on the opening; discovery still works.

## Flag for the guide owner
- If the other 12 skills had their descriptions reduced to the bare "A friendly
  assistant that helps with <topic>" template, they may be under-triggering.
  Worth checking whether their descriptions still carry "use when" triggers.
  Happy to audit them if useful.
