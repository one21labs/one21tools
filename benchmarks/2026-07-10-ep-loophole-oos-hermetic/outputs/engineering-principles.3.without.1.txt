I'd hold off on building the plugin framework right now. With exactly one exporter, you don't yet know what actually varies between implementations — the abstraction you design today is a guess, and guessed abstractions tend to need painful rework once a second real case shows up and doesn't fit the assumed shape. The standard move is: build the second exporter as a normal addition, then extract the common interface from the two real examples you have.

If you want, I can instead take a quick look at the current exporter to flag where it's already doing things that would make a second exporter awkward to add (hardcoded assumptions, missing seams) — that's useful prep without committing to a framework design prematurely.
